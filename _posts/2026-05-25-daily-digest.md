**[Energy per Successful Goal: Goal-Level Energy Accounting for Agentic AI Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22883)**  
# Energy per Successful Goal: A Smarter Way to Measure AI Efficiency

Researchers have unveiled A-LEMS, a breakthrough framework that shifts how we measure AI energy consumption—moving from per-inference metrics to "Energy per Successful Goal," which captures the true cost of agentic systems that retry, self-correct, and orchestrate multiple steps to solve real problems. This reframing exposes a critical blind spot in current benchmarks: traditional metrics hide the actual energy footprint of intelligent agents working through complex tasks, making way for more honest accountability and smarter optimization. It's a deceptively simple idea that could reshape how the industry builds and deploys more efficient AI systems at scale.

## arXiv CS.AI

**[BOHM: Zero-Cost Hierarchical Attribution for Compound AI Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22866)**  
# BOHM: Zero-Cost Hierarchical Attribution for Compound AI Systems

BOHM solves a critical blind spot in modern AI systems: understanding *which* specialized components actually drive results in complex, multi-stage AI pipelines—without requiring expensive or impossible test runs. By extracting attribution directly from existing routing weights that orchestrators already track, this method finally makes explainability practical for real-world compound AI systems, from API chains to agentic workflows, opening the door to faster debugging, optimization, and trustworthiness at scale.
